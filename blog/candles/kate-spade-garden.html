Kate Spade Bon Voyage - Garden

{candles, reviews}

03-24-21

overview

This is probably the candle that got me into enjoying expensive candles for the first time. I remember my mom bought this candle (and one called Bazaar) from our local TJ Maxx where it was hidden in the candle aisle. I'm a high school student, so I don't do too much candle-hunting on my own time, but my mom likes to go out and try to find luxury candles that have been put out to resell. I can put it here because no one will ever read it, but there are few things in life that make me as excited as when my mom comes home from shopping and tells me that she swiped a new candle at 1/10th of its original price.

specs

This 10 oz candle made of soy and paraffin wax came in its original box. It has a single wick and a plastic dust cover for the top of the candle. The inside of the vessel was pink, which fit with the rest of the candle's theme.

Brand // Name:

Kate Spade Bon Voyage - Garden

Scents:

Sweet cassis, pink peony, and buttery jasmine

Wick // Wax:

? // Soy and paraffin

60 hours

rating  7/10

This rates at a 7 out of 10 because I thought the scent wasn't 100% to my liking, even though it was more unique. This candle smelled light and airy, which is my best way of describing its scent without saying the typical "it smelled like flowers." It didn't smell like roses, although I don't think I've ever smelled a rose before, so I can't really say. I think it smelled like peonies. The vessel was cute and I burned the candle to the end so that I could reuse it as a pen holder - it's on my desk right now! There was no tunneling, although the edges of the vessel gathered some soot by the time I had used up the entire candle. I probably wouldn't buy this candle because of how expensive it is, but if I had the chance to buy another discounted one of these, I definitely would.
